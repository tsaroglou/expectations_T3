Groups:

Each participant in role B (henceforth, “B participant”) is linked to 4 participants in role A (henceforth, “A participants”), as illustrated below:

Note that, although groups always contain five participants, the total number of groups in each session might vary depending on how many people participate. Each session includes between 2 and 4 groups.

Initial situation:

There are three boxes: the blue box, the green box, and the red box. One of these three boxes contains money: €12 for participant A and €10 for participant B. The other two are empty.

With 25% probability the box that contains the money is locked and cannot be opened. This means that with 25% probability the box that contains the money will leave both participants with €0.

If no box is chosen (participants “stay out”), the B participant earns €6 and each A participant earns €4.

At the beginning, no participant knows which of the three boxes contains the money. They know that each box has the same probability of containing the money.

Note: Which box contains the money may differ across A participants.

The A participants who scored in the top 50% in the test find out which box contains the money. Those who did not score in the top 50% do not find out. In case of ties, ties will be broken based on test completion time.

B participants never find out which box contains the money, and never find out which A participants scored in the top 50% in the test.

There will be three steps:

Step 1: Every B participant must decide whether they want to stay out or let the A participants they are linked to (henceforth, “their A participants”) make a decision.

If a B participant decides to let their A participants make a decision, one of them will be randomly selected and their decision will determine the payment for the B participant.

Step 2: Each A participant learns whether they scored in the top 50% in the test or not. If they scored in the top 50%, they immediately find out which box contains the money.

If they did not score in the top 50%, they do not find out. B participants never find out which box contains the money, and they never find out which A participants scored in the top 50% in the test.

Step 3: Without knowing the choice made by their B participant in Step 1, each A participant must decide whether they want to stay out or choose a box.

Their B participant will observe this decision and the resulting payoff.

Specifically, for each of their A participants, a B participant will observe one of the following options:

  • A chooses a box and B gets €10
  • A chooses a box and B gets €0
  • A stays out and B gets €6

Note: The decision made by each A participant will be implemented only if their B participant has decided to let them make a choice in Step 1.

If their B participant has decided to stay out, the decision made by the A participants will be inconsequential.

Note: All interactions between the B participant and the four A participants will be anonymous.
